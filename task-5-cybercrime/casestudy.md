# Case Study — UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Overview

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes targeting college
students and young adults in India. This case study describes a realistic
scenario based on commonly reported incidents.

## The Scenario

Rahul, a first-year engineering student, received a WhatsApp message from
an unknown number claiming to be from his bank. The message stated that his
UPI account would be blocked unless he verified his details immediately.
A link was provided which opened a page that looked exactly like his bank's
official website.

## How It Happened — Step by Step

1. Rahul received a fake urgent message creating panic.
2. He clicked the phishing link which led to a fake bank website.
3. He entered his UPI PIN and account details on the fake page.
4. Within minutes, Rs. 15,000 was transferred out of his account.
5. The fraudster disappeared and the fake website went offline.

## Who Is Usually Targeted

- College students with new bank accounts or UPI apps
- People who are not aware of phishing techniques
- Individuals who panic when they receive urgent-sounding messages

## Consequences

- Financial loss that is often unrecoverable
- Mental stress and anxiety
- Compromised personal and banking information
- Risk of identity theft if more details were shared
